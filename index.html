April 12-13, 1861

Fort Sumpter

Fort Sumpter, located near Charleston, South Carolina, was attacked by the South Carolina Militia, as this was before the Confederate army. The reason they were attacking a fort in their own state was because it was occupied by the United States Army, who then returned fire. The bombardment started at 4:30, where a mortar was shot from Fort Johnson. The mortar then exploded over Sumter, which was the signal for the rest of their militia to begin the attack. The bombardment lasted until 1pm the next day, when they the United States army surrendered. This caused the start of the Civil War

July 21, 1861

First Battle of Bull Run

The battle which was called the First Battle of Bull Run by the Union forces, and the First Battle of Manassas by the Confederate army, was the first major battle of the Civil War. It was fought in Prince William County, Virginia. The Union army to a long time to prepare, which allowed the Confederate Army to receive more soldiers via railway.
